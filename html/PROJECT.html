YOU MATTER

Your mental illness is not a personal failure
A mental illness is a condition that affects a person's thinking, feeling or mood. Such conditions may affect someone's ability to relate to others and function each day. Each person will have different experiences, even people with the same diagnosis. Recovery, including meaningful roles in social life, school and work, IS possible, especially when you start treatment early and play a strong role in YOUR OWN recovery process.

Depression

10 COMMON SYMPTOMS:
1. Feelings of helplessness and hopelessness.
2. Loss of interest in daily activities.
3. Appetite or weight changes.
4. Sleep changes.
5. Anger or irritability.
6. Loss of energy.
7. Self-loathing.
8. Reckless behavior.
9. Concentration problems.
10. Unexplained aches and pains.

Anxiety

10 COMMON SYMPTOMS:
1. nervousness, restlessness, or being tense
2. feelings of danger, panic, or dread
3. rapid heart rate
4. rapid breathing, or hyperventilation
5. increased or heavy sweating
6. trembling or muscle twitching
7. weakness and lethargy
8. difficulty focusing or thinking clearly about anything
other than the thing you’re worried about
9. insomnia
10. digestive or gastrointestinal problems,
such as gas, constipation, or diarrhea

Eating Disorder

10 COMMON SYMPTOMS:
1. Constant adherence to increasingly strict diets, regardless of weight
2. Habitual trips to the bathroom immediately after eating
3. Secretly bingeing on large amounts of food
4. Hoarding large amounts of food
5. Increase in consumption of laxatives, diuretics or diet pills
6. Exercising compulsively, often several hours per day
7. Using prescription stimulant medications and/or illicit stimulant drugs to
suppress appetite
8. Withdrawal from friends and family
9. Avoidance of meals or situations where food may be present
10. Preoccupation with weight, body size and shape.

Bipolar

10 COMMON SYMPTOMS:
1. heightened sense of self-importance
2. exaggerated positive outlook
3. significantly decreased need for sleep
4. poor appetite and weight loss
5. racing speech, flight of ideas, impulsiveness
6. feelings of sadness or hopelessness
7. loss of interest in pleasurable or usual activities
8. difficulty sleeping; early-morning awakening
9. loss of energy and constant lethargy
10. sense of guilt or low self-esteem
